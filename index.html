For host families

Your AuPair is arriving from India.
Are you both ready?

Most families think they are prepared. They have children, they have experience. But receiving someone from a completely different culture — without preparation — is where things go wrong. For everyone, especially the children.

What families never think about — until it's too late

Childcare differencesIndependent play is not a concept in Indian childcare culture. Children are rarely left alone — even for a moment.
Food & mealtimesGerman portion sizes, meal structure, and food habits are completely foreign. Someone needs to explain — before day one.
Household appliancesA dishwasher, a Thermomix, a dryer — familiar to you. Completely unknown to someone arriving from India.
Communication styleGerman directness is often misread as anger or criticism. Without preparation, misunderstandings escalate into conflict.

A prepared AuPair and a prepared family means stable, happy children — which is what everyone actually wants.

What is Ausbildung?

Germany's most powerful pathway —
and most AuPairs miss it

Ausbildung is Germany's vocational training system — a government-recognised, paid programme where you train on the job in a company while attending vocational school. At the end, you hold a German qualification that opens doors for life.

€900–1,150
Monthly training pay
2–3 years
Duration + qualification
Legal visa
Path to stay long-term
You already have an advantage
You are already in Germany. You understand the culture. You speak some German. You have a German reference. Employers see this — and it matters more than you think.
Timing is everything
Most Ausbildung programmes start in September or February. If you are in your AuPair year now, the window to apply is open. Miss it and you wait another year — or go home.
